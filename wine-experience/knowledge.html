Wine Knowledge

Learn about wine and test your knowledge

Wine Basics

Learn about different wine types, production methods, and basic tasting techniques.

Types of Wine

There are five basic types of wine: red, white, rosé, sparkling, and dessert. Each has distinct characteristics based on the grapes used and production methods.

Wine Production

Wine production involves harvesting grapes, crushing them, fermenting the juice, aging the wine, and finally bottling it. Each step influences the final flavor.

Wine Tasting

Wine tasting involves examining a wine's appearance, smelling its aromas, and tasting it to evaluate its flavors, body, and finish.

Wine Regions

Discover the major wine regions of the world and what makes each unique.

France

France is home to famous regions like Bordeaux, Burgundy, and Champagne. Each region specializes in certain grape varieties and has distinct terroir.

Italy

Italy boasts more grape varieties than any other country, with regions like Tuscany, Piedmont, and Veneto producing world-renowned wines.

New World Regions

Regions like Napa Valley (USA), Mendoza (Argentina), and Marlborough (New Zealand) have made their mark with distinctive wines and innovative techniques.

Food and Wine Pairing

Master the art of pairing wines with food to enhance both culinary experiences.

Basic Principles

The basic principle of wine pairing is to match the weight and intensity of food with a similarly weighted wine.

Red Wine Pairings

Red wines typically pair well with red meats, hearty pasta dishes, and strong cheeses.

White Wine Pairings

White wines often complement fish, chicken, and lighter vegetable dishes.

Wine Terminology Glossary

Explore common wine terms to enhance your wine vocabulary.

Acidity
The tart or sour taste in wine that makes it refreshing and gives it structure.
Body
The perception of weight and fullness of a wine in your mouth (light, medium, or full).
Bouquet
The complex aromas that develop in wines as they age.
Finish
The taste and sensations that linger after swallowing wine.
Legs
The streams of wine that run down the side of a glass after swirling, indicating alcohol content.
Tannins
Compounds that create a drying sensation in the mouth, mainly found in red wines.
Terroir
The complete natural environment in which a wine is produced, including soil, climate, and topography.
Vintage
The year in which a wine's grapes were harvested.
Sommelier
A trained wine professional who specializes in all aspects of wine service and food pairing.
Appellation
A legally defined and protected geographical indication used to identify where grapes for a wine were grown.
